Olympic National Park, Washington

Once the old civilization has consumed itself from sickening greed and ignorance,
those remaining will have learned this lesson the hard way.
That the sun does not revolve around the Earth and God did not make us in their image alone.
That the Earth was not made for our benefit, but for us to protect it. And we will learn that we had failed.

Outlands

Retreat to the outlands
where the grid's reach halts, and the grass grows green.
Out where there lives no man,
and we won't be held to the hell of man's greed.
Now escape no one can.
Of man's legacy? there will be no memories.

Krakatoa

fissures spew molten confetti
to celebrate the end of man's reign.
encroaching plumes of relentless soot clouds
smother and silence even the Gods' cries
who recoil from the visceral stings of
cracking lightning lashes:
brilliant flashes formed from nature's spite.
And our Gods flee,

to leave us

to the mercy of Krakatoa.
fiery vermillion ignites the starless sky.
a fortress of growing wrath nurtures suffocating clouds,
leaving the ground below colder
and darker
as the only god I know whips fragmented shards towards hell.
blood and black snow rain down.
run now.

Falling Cradle

When the wind blows
the cradle falls.
Will it be enough
once you've had it all?
Fill your husk
with misery.
Plead and cry
but you haven't changed.
Expect no grace once it comes undone.
